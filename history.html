History of Alash Orda

Exploring the roots and legacy of Alash Orda in Kazakh history.

Overview

Alash Orda emerged in the early 20th century as a pivotal movement advocating for the autonomy and cultural rights of the Kazakh people within the Tsarist Russian Empire and later amidst the tumultuous backdrop of the Russian Revolution. Named after Alash, the legendary ancestor of the Kazakhs, this political and cultural movement was led by a group of visionary intellectuals and activists. They aspired to establish a democratic framework that honored the unique heritage and traditions of the Kazakh people while navigating the complexities of a rapidly changing political landscape. The ideals and efforts of Alash Orda played a critical role in shaping modern Kazakh national identity and laid the groundwork for future quests for sovereignty and self-governance.

Significant Figures

Alikhan Bokeikhanov

A Kazakh writer, political activist and environmental scientist. He is famous for advocating the idea that Kazakhs should learn Russian culture and simultaneously preserve Kazakh customs and law.

Akhmet Baytursynuly

A Kazakh intellectual who worked in the fields of politic, poetry, linguistics and education. Baitursynov was a leading figure in the Alash Orda government and a champion of education and cultural reform.

Mirjaqip Dulatuli

A Kazakh poet, writer and one of leaders of Kazakh nationalist Alash Orda government. He was known for his passionate advocacy for the Kazakh people.

Impact and Legacy of the Alash Movement

The Alash movement, as a national liberation struggle against the Russian Empire's colonial policies in the early 20th century, played a significant role in shaping the political and cultural landscape of Kazakhstan. Its inception during the tumultuous period of the First Russian Revolution laid the foundation for a persistent fight for national autonomy and self-governance amidst growing challenges.

Formation and Ideals

The Karkaralin petition of 1905 and the consequential Delegate conference in Uralsk marked the early formation of the Alash movement. The main agenda was to address the grievances of the Kazakh people against the increasing settlement of immigrants and the usurpation of local lands. The fundamental aim was to establish a Kazakh national party, later evolving into the "Alash" party, advocating for national unity, cultural preservation, and self-governance.

Political and Social Advocacy

Post the October Russian Revolution, Alash leaders such as A.Bokeikhanov, A.Baitursynov, and M.Shokay intensified their efforts to promote Kazakh societal development. Their work included significant contributions to education, journalism, and political mobilization, emphasizing the need for a Kazakh national state and preservation of traditional Kazakh lifestyles.

Alash Autonomy and Legacy

The First All-Kazak Congress in Orenburg in July 1917 was a milestone, asserting the Kazakhs' right to territorial-national autonomy within a democratic Russian Republic. Despite challenges post-October Revolution, the Alash movement continued to advocate for the rights and freedoms of the Kazakh people. The establishment of local governmental bodies and efforts towards socio-economic development underscored their commitment to national rejuvenation.

Enduring Influence

Although the Alash movement faced suppression by Bolshevik forces, its principles and goals continued to resonate. The movement's advocacy for national unity, education in the native language, and preservation of cultural identity have left an indelible mark on Kazakhstan's history. The legacy of the Alash movement is evident in the continued struggle for national identity and autonomy in Kazakhstan, inspiring future generations in their pursuit of self-determination and cultural integrity.

Conclusion

In the face of adversity and political upheaval, the Alash movement stands as a testament to the resilience and determination of the Kazakh people. Its impact on shaping the national consciousness and fostering a sense of unity continues to be a source of inspiration and pride for Kazakhstan.
